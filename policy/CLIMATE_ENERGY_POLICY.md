# CLIMATE & ENERGY POLICY
## The Abundant Energy Future

## Executive Summary

Climate change is real. The response must be equally real—not virtue signaling, not regulatory theater, but **massive deployment of abundant, clean energy**. The Zeno approach rejects both climate denial and climate alarmism that opposes nuclear power and technological solutions.

**Core Principle:** The path to zero emissions runs through abundance, not scarcity. We solve climate change by making clean energy so cheap and plentiful that fossil fuels become economically obsolete.

**The Zeno Energy Framework:**
- **Solar and Wind:** Already cost-competitive, deploy at maximum speed
- **Nuclear Fission:** Small Modular Reactors as baseload backbone
- **Nuclear Fusion:** Aggressive investment for commercial deployment by 2035
- **AI Grid Optimization:** Intelligent systems to maximize efficiency
- **Carbon Pricing:** Market mechanisms to accelerate transition
- **No Degrowth:** Reject anti-human policies that restrict prosperity

## Part I: The Energy Reality

### Current State of Global Energy

**The Problem:**
- Global CO2 emissions: ~37 billion metric tons annually
- Energy sector responsibility: ~75% of global greenhouse gas emissions
- Fossil fuel dependency: Still provides ~80% of world energy
- Climate trajectory: Current policies lead to 2.5-3°C warming by 2100

**The Opportunity:**
- Renewable costs have fallen 90% in 15 years
- Solar and wind now cheapest new electricity in most markets
- Nuclear technology advancing rapidly
- Fusion approaching commercial viability
- AI can optimize grids to reduce waste 12-15%

### Why Technology Wins

The climate debate is dominated by two failed approaches:

| Approach | Problem |
|----------|---------|
| **Denial** | Ignores physical reality, delays necessary action |
| **Degrowth** | Anti-human, politically impossible, economically devastating |

**The Zeno Alternative:** Technology-driven abundance. Make clean energy so cheap that choosing fossil fuels becomes economically irrational. Don't restrict prosperity—expand it sustainably.

Historical precedent: The transition from whale oil to petroleum wasn't mandated by government decree. Petroleum was simply better and cheaper. Clean energy must follow the same path.

## Part III: Nuclear Fission Renaissance

### The Case for Nuclear

Nuclear power is:
- **Zero-carbon** during operation
- **Energy-dense** (1 million times fossil fuels per unit mass)
- **Reliable** 24/7 baseload power
- **Safe** (lowest deaths per TWh of any energy source)
- **Land-efficient** (smallest footprint per MW)

**The Anti-Nuclear Fallacy:**
Opposition to nuclear power is scientifically illiterate and climate-destructive. Germany's nuclear phase-out increased emissions. California's plant closures increased fossil fuel dependency. The Zeno platform rejects ideological opposition to nuclear energy.

### Small Modular Reactors (SMRs): The Game Changer

**Current Status (2025):**
- 127 SMR designs globally, 7 operating or under construction
- NuScale VOYGR is only NRC-licensed SMR in U.S.
- Major tech companies investing: Amazon ($700M in X-energy), Google (Kairos Power), Microsoft (Three Mile Island restart)
- Trump administration goal: Quadruple nuclear capacity to 400 GW by 2050

**Why SMRs Win:**

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Factory fabrication** | Built in factories, shipped to sites, reducing costs |
| **Passive safety** | Walk-away safe designs without operator intervention |
| **Flexible siting** | Smaller footprint, can replace retiring coal plants |
| **Grid flexibility** | Can load-follow, complementing renewables |
| **Reduced financing risk** | Smaller upfront capital, shorter construction |

### Nuclear Fission Policy Framework

**The American Nuclear Renaissance Act**

**Pillar 1: Licensing Reform**
- NRC licensing timeline: 24 months maximum for approved designs
- Generic design certification for SMR families
- Pre-approved sites at retiring coal and gas plants
- Standardized safety protocols reducing duplication

**Pillar 2: Manufacturing & Supply Chain**
- Tax credits for domestic SMR manufacturing
- HALEU fuel production capacity expansion (critical bottleneck)
- Workforce development: 10,000 nuclear engineers annually
- National Nuclear Supply Chain Initiative

**Pillar 3: Deployment Incentives**

| Incentive | Value |

| Production Tax Credit | $30/MWh for first 10 years |
| Investment Tax Credit | 30% of capital costs |
| Loan guarantees | Up to 80% of project cost |

**Pillar 4: Waste Solutions**
- Restart Yucca Mountain licensing process
- Interim consolidated storage facilities
- Research funding for advanced reprocessing
- Geological repository for long-lived waste

**Deployment Targets:**

| Year | SMR Capacity | Total Nuclear |

| 2030 | 10 GW | 110 GW |

## Part VI: Carbon Pricing

### The Economic Case for Carbon Pricing

Carbon pricing internalizes the externality of emissions. Without a price on carbon, polluters don't pay the true cost of their activities—society does.

**Global Status (2025):**
- 80 carbon pricing instruments worldwide
- 28% of global emissions covered
- $100+ billion in annual revenue generated
- Proven effectiveness: EU ETS cut covered emissions 50% since 2005

**Carbon Price Requirements:**
According to UN analysis, achieving Paris targets requires:
- $50-100/ton by 2030
- $130-350/ton by 2050

Current average global carbon price: ~$23/ton (far too low)

### Carbon Pricing Policy Framework

**The American Carbon Dividend Act**

**Core Mechanism: Fee and Dividend**

| Component | Design |
|-----------|--------|
| **Carbon Fee** | Levied at point of extraction/import |
| **Starting Rate** | $40/ton CO2 equivalent |
| **Annual Increase** | $10/ton until emissions targets met |
| **Revenue Disposition** | 100% returned to citizens as dividend |
| **Border Adjustment** | Applies to imports from non-pricing countries |

**Why Fee and Dividend:**
1. **Revenue neutral:** Not a tax increase—all money returned
2. **Progressive:** Lower-income households receive more than they pay
3. **Market-based:** Lets market determine optimal decarbonization path
4. **Bipartisan appeal:** Supported by economists left and right
5. **No government growth:** No new programs, bureaucracies, or spending

**Projected Impacts:**

| Year | Carbon Fee | Dividend (family of 4) | Emissions Reduction |
|------|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 2027 | $40/ton | ~$2,000/year | 5% below baseline |
| 2030 | $70/ton | ~$3,500/year | 20% below baseline |
| 2035 | $120/ton | ~$5,000/year | 40% below baseline |
| 2040 | $150/ton | ~$4,500/year* | 60% below baseline |

*Dividend decreases as emissions (and therefore revenue) decline

**Border Carbon Adjustment:**
- Imports from countries without equivalent carbon pricing face border fee
- Exports to those countries receive rebate
- Prevents carbon leakage (production moving to non-pricing countries)
- Incentivizes global carbon pricing adoption

### Integration with Zeno Economic Framework

Carbon dividend complements but is **separate from** Robot Tax/Data Tax funded UBI:

| Revenue Source | Purpose | Mechanism |
|----------------|---------|-----------|
| Carbon Fee | Climate transition | Dividend to all citizens |
| Automation Tax | Automation dividend | UBI supplement |
| Data Tax | Data dividend | UBI supplement |

The carbon dividend provides additional household income while driving decarbonization. As emissions decline, the carbon dividend decreases while automation/data dividends increase—a smooth transition to the post-carbon, post-labor economy.
