# COMMUNITY INFRASTRUCTURE
## Building Meaning Beyond Work
### The Human Standard

---

## Executive Summary

Work provides more than income. It provides structure, social connection, identity, and purpose. As automation makes traditional employment optional for more people, we must intentionally build the infrastructure that supports human flourishing beyond the workplace.

**The Core Principle**: Citizen's Royalty replaces income. It does not automatically replace meaning. We must invest in the institutions and spaces that enable human connection, contribution, and purpose outside of traditional employment.

---

## Part I: What Work Provides (Beyond Income)

### The Functions of Work

| Function | What Work Provides | Risk Without It |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Structure** | Daily routine, time organization | Drift, depression, purposelessness |
| **Social contact** | Colleagues, workplace relationships | Isolation, loneliness |
| **Identity** | "I am a [profession]" | Identity crisis, loss of self-worth |
| **Status** | Position, recognition | Status anxiety, social comparison |
| **Purpose** | Contribution to something larger | Meaninglessness, existential distress |
| **Mastery** | Skill development, competence | Stagnation, boredom |
| **Activity** | Something to do each day | Inertia, depression |

### The Risk of Citizen's Royalty Without Meaning Infrastructure

Evidence from early retirees, lottery winners, and trust fund recipients shows that income without purpose often leads to:
- Increased depression and anxiety
- Substance abuse
- Relationship breakdown
- Shortened lifespan
- Reported lower life satisfaction despite material comfort

Citizen's Royalty is necessary but not sufficient for human flourishing.

## Part III: Community Centers

### Vision

Every American within 15 minutes of a vibrant community center offering activities, services, connection, and purpose.

### Model: The 21st Century Community Center

**Physical Space**:
- Meeting rooms (free to reserve)
- Workshop spaces (woodworking, crafts, maker spaces)
- Kitchen facilities (community meals, cooking classes)
- Childcare rooms
- Quiet spaces (reading, meditation, co-working)
- Outdoor gathering areas

**Programming**:
- Classes and skill-sharing (taught by community members)
- Support groups (facilitated peer support)
- Community meals (weekly gathering)
- Intergenerational programs (youth-elder connection)
- Local governance forums
- Cultural events and performances

**Services**:
- Social services navigation
- Mental health first-responders
- Financial counseling
- Job/opportunity boards (for those who want to work)
- Health screening and referrals

### Implementation

| Timeline | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Year 1-2 | Pilot 100 centers in high-displacement regions |
| Year 3-5 | Expand to 1,000 centers nationwide |
| Year 5-10 | Full buildout: 10,000+ centers (1 per 30,000 residents) |

### Governance

- Local community boards (elected)
- Federal funding with local control
- Required accessibility standards
- Outcome reporting (participation, satisfaction, community health metrics)

### Cost

| Phase | Capital | Annual Operating |
|-------|---------|------------------|
| Pilot (100 centers) | $2B | $500M |
| Expansion (1,000 centers) | $15B | $3B |
| Full buildout (10,000 centers) | $50B | $10B |

## Part IV: National Service Corps

### Vision

Meaningful service opportunities available to all Americans, compensated above Citizen's Royalty, providing purpose, community, and contribution.

### Core Programs

| Corps | Focus | Typical Assignment |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **Conservation Corps** | Environmental restoration, parks, public lands | Trail building, habitat restoration, fire prevention |
| **Care Corps** | Eldercare, childcare, disability support | In-home assistance, respite care, companionship |
| **Education Corps** | Tutoring, mentoring, classroom support | After-school programs, adult literacy, STEM mentorship |
| **Civic Corps** | Community organizing, local governance support | Neighborhood revitalization, democratic participation |
| **Emergency Corps** | Disaster response, community resilience | Preparedness training, disaster deployment |
| **Arts Corps** | Public art, cultural programming, creative placemaking | Murals, performances, community cultural events |

### Eligibility and Terms

- **Age**: 18+ (no upper limit—seniors welcome)
- **Term**: 6 months to 2 years
- **Hours**: Full-time (35 hours/week) or part-time (15-20 hours/week)
- **Compensation**: $30,000/year full-time (above Citizen's Royalty, not replacing it)

### Benefits

| Benefit | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Living stipend | $30,000/year (full-time) |
| Healthcare | Full coverage during service |
| Education award | $10,000 for each year of service |
| Student loan forgiveness | $5,000 per year applied to federal loans |
| Retirement credit | Service years count toward Social Security |

### Scale

| Year | Participants | Annual Cost |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| 1-2 | 100,000 | $5B |
| 3-5 | 500,000 | $20B |
| 5-10 | 1,000,000 | $40B |

Target: 3% of adult population participating at any time.

### Not Mandatory

- Purely voluntary
- No penalty for not participating
- Citizen's Royalty available regardless
- Service is an opportunity, not an obligation

## Part V: Public Spaces

### Vision

Abundant, high-quality public spaces that enable gathering, recreation, and connection.

### Investment Areas

| Category | Investment | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Parks expansion | $30B | Urban parks, pocket parks, regional parks |
| Libraries transformation | $20B | Modernize into community hubs, extended hours |
| Public plazas | $15B | Pedestrian spaces, town squares, gathering areas |
| Recreation facilities | $20B | Pools, gyms, sports fields, courts |
| Natural areas access | $15B | Trail networks, waterfront access, conservation lands |

### Design Principles

- **Welcoming**: No hostile architecture, benches that can be sat on, shade
- **Programmable**: Spaces that support events, gatherings, performances
- **All-weather**: Covered areas, heated spaces in cold climates
- **Accessible**: ADA compliant, transit accessible, in all neighborhoods
- **Safe**: Well-lit, maintained, programmed (activity creates safety)

### "Third Place" Initiative

Fund the creation of "third places"—neither home nor work—where community happens:
- Coffee shops with community space
- Pubs with meeting rooms (yes, really)
- Recreation clubs
- Religious/secular gathering spaces

$5B in grants to support local third place development.

## Part VI: Civic Technology

### Vision

Digital infrastructure that enables democratic participation, community organizing, and collective decision-making.

### Platforms

| Platform | Purpose | Features |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **CivicConnect** | Local issue discussion and voting | Neighborhood forums, local voting, issue tracking |
| **MeetLocal** | Event and gathering coordination | Free event hosting, interest matching, space booking |
| **VolunteerMatch** | Service opportunity matching | Skills-based matching, scheduling, impact tracking |
| **TimeBank** | Skill and time exchange | Non-monetary exchange, community currency |
| **LocalGov** | Local government transparency | Budget tracking, meeting access, official communication |

### Principles

- **Open source**: All platforms publicly auditable
- **Privacy-respecting**: Minimal data collection, user control
- **Interoperable**: Works with other platforms, no lock-in
- **Accessible**: Works on low-end devices, supports multiple languages

### Investment

$10B over 10 years for development and deployment
$5B/year for operations and support

## Part VIII: Arts and Culture

### Vision

A thriving creative ecosystem where artists can pursue their work and communities have access to rich cultural experiences.

### Programs

| Program | Description | Annual Cost |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Artist Basic Income** | Supplemental income for working artists | $5B |
| **Public Art Commission** | Art in public spaces | $3B |
| **Community Arts Grants** | Support local arts organizations | $4B |
| **Free Museum/Performance Access** | Subsidized access to cultural institutions | $2B |
| **Arts in Schools** | Restore arts education | $4B |
| **Cultural Preservation** | Document and preserve local cultures | $2B |

### Artist Basic Income

A supplemental $1,000/month for practicing artists (in addition to Citizen's Royalty):
- Eligibility: Demonstrated artistic practice (portfolio, performance history)
- Not means-tested (Citizen's Royalty provides the floor)
- Reviewed every 3 years for continued practice

Purpose: Enable artistic pursuit as a legitimate life path, not just for the wealthy.

## Part IX: Integration with Citizen's Royalty

### The Combined Vision

| Source | Monthly Amount | Purpose |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| Citizen's Royalty | $800-1,200 | Basic economic security |
| National Service (if participating) | +$2,500 | Contribution compensation |
| Artist Basic Income (if eligible) | +$1,000 | Creative pursuit support |
| Community center access | Free | Connection and activity |
| Lifelong learning | Free | Growth and engagement |
| Public spaces | Free | Recreation and gathering |

A person can have economic security, meaningful activity, continuous learning, and community connection—without traditional employment.

### The Choice Architecture

We're not prescribing how people should live. We're providing options:

- Want to work? Traditional employment remains available and rewarded
- Want to serve? National Service Corps provides purpose and extra income
- Want to create? Artist Basic Income and community resources support you
- Want to learn? Free education available
- Want to care for family? Citizen's Royalty enables unpaid care work
- Want to do nothing? That's your right, and basic needs are met

## Part X: Addressing the "Lazy" Concern

### The Evidence

Every study of financial security shows people don't become "lazy":

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Finland Citizen's Royalty | No decrease in employment, improved wellbeing |
| Germany Citizen's Royalty | No work reduction, increased job satisfaction |
| Kenya GiveDirectly | Increased entrepreneurship, not idleness |
| Stockton SEED | Full-time employment increased |
| Alaska PFD | No idleness epidemic in 40+ years |

### Why Humans Are Not "Lazy"

- **Social pressure**: Humans care about status and contribution
- **Boredom**: Most people don't enjoy doing nothing
- **Purpose-seeking**: Humans naturally seek meaning
- **Financial motivation**: Citizen's Royalty is a floor, not a ceiling—work still pays

### The Small Minority

Yes, some people will choose to do very little. This is:
- A small minority (evidence suggests <5%)
- Their choice to make in a free society
- Less costly than the surveillance/enforcement alternative
- Possibly reflecting depression, disability, or caregiving (deserving compassion, not judgment)

## Conclusion

Money is necessary for human flourishing, but it is not sufficient. As work becomes optional, we must intentionally build the infrastructure that work once provided: structure, connection, identity, purpose.

The meaning infrastructure investment is not welfare—it is the essential complement to Citizen's Royalty that makes it work. Without it, Citizen's Royalty risks creating a population of isolated, purposeless individuals. With it, Citizen's Royalty becomes liberation—freedom to pursue meaning however you define it.

We are not creating a society of idleness. We are creating a society of choice—where economic security enables, rather than prevents, human flourishing.
