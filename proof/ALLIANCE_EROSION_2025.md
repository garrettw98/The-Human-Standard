### Key Facts

- The US threatened **military force against a NATO ally** (Denmark) for the first time in NATO history.
- **Tariffs imposed on every major ally**: Canada, Mexico, EU, Japan, South Korea, UK, Australia — then **struck down by the Supreme Court** (Feb 20, 2026) as exceeding IEEPA authority.
- Average effective tariff rate hit **27%** — highest since the 1890s, amounting to ~$1,500/household.
- NATO allies were **explicitly excluded** from Ukraine peace negotiations that would reshape European security.
- Trump demands **5% of GDP** NATO spending — a "deliberate impossibility" designed to justify withdrawal.
- The National Security Strategy described US posture as **"predisposition to non-interventionism"** and referenced Europe's **"civilizational decline."**
- Extended nuclear deterrence — the foundation of allied security since 1945 — was **not explicitly reaffirmed**.
- Gulf allies were used as **staging areas for Iran strikes**, then **hit by Iranian retaliation** they didn't sign up for.
- EU launches **€800 billion "ReArm Europe"** program — explicitly to reduce US dependence.
- **Canada opens trade talks with China** — the US tariff war pushes its closest ally toward its primary competitor.
- **3,000 US troops withdrawn from Romania** — on the Ukrainian border — during an active war in Europe.

## PREDICTION-BY-PREDICTION ANALYSIS

### 1. Greenland — Threatening a NATO Ally

**What we said:** THE COUNTER-COUP identified the NRx worldview as treating the US as *"a 'legacy corporation' to be liquidated"* and warned that *"Thiel and Musk are globalists in the truest sense—they have no loyalty to the US Constitution."* DEFENSE_FOREIGN_POLICY.md committed to NATO as a cornerstone of security strategy.

**What happened:** The President threatened military force against Denmark — a founding NATO member — to seize Greenland. This triggered an existential debate about whether Article 5 (mutual defense) still means anything. Simultaneously, Thiel-backed Praxis ($525M) had been exploring Greenland as a site for a network state "freedom city," raising questions about whether the geopolitical push and the private venture are aligned.

**Assessment:** Threatening a NATO ally with force is unprecedented in the alliance's 75-year history. It fundamentally undermines the credibility of collective defense. **Confirmed.**

---

### 2. Trade War Against Every Ally Simultaneously

**What we said:** DEFENSE_FOREIGN_POLICY.md structured the entire defense framework around alliance partnerships — NATO, Indo-Pacific bilateral treaties, Five Eyes, AUKUS, Quad. The core principle: *"Alliances multiply strength."*

**What happened:** The administration imposed tariffs on **every major ally**: 25% on Canada and Mexico, 25% on Japanese and Korean autos, Section 232 on EU steel/aluminum, 10% on 8 NATO allies specifically. The average effective tariff rate hit 27% — a level not seen since the 1890s. Canada's economy contracted 1.6% in one quarter from the shock. The EU was told to buy $350B in US energy or face further penalties.

This is not selective pressure on a specific issue. This is economic warfare against the entire alliance network simultaneously.

**Assessment:** **Confirmed.**

---

### 3. Ukraine — Excluding Allies from Their Own Security

**What we said:** DEFENSE_FOREIGN_POLICY.md committed to *"Diplomatic engagement... Manage competition to avoid war"* and alliance-based approaches to Russia.

**What happened:** The 28-point Ukraine peace plan was developed without meaningful input from Ukraine or European allies — the nations with the most at stake. NATO allies explicitly opposed the plan. European capitals described it as rewarding Russian aggression and undermining the rules-based order Europe depends on.

Simultaneously, the National Security Strategy emphasized a *"predisposition to non-interventionism"* — signaling that the US may not come to Europe's defense. The 2026 National Defense Strategy failed to explicitly commit to extended nuclear deterrence.

**Assessment:** When the US builds a European security deal without Europeans, it is not diplomacy. It is imposition. The alliance framework predicted consultation-based approaches; the reality is unilateral decree. **Confirmed.**

---

### 4. Gulf Allies — Staging Areas, Not Partners

**What we said:** DEFENSE_FOREIGN_POLICY.md committed to *"Partner Security Architecture"* with Qatar, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, and others (Part XI, Pillar 2). THE COUNTER-COUP emphasized building a *"Humanist Front"* that includes alliance partners.

**What happened:** US strikes on Iran were launched from bases in Gulf partner nations without meaningful consultation. Iran retaliated by striking targets in all of them — airports, refineries, hotels, residential areas. Saudi Arabia shut its largest refinery. Qatar halted all LNG production. At least 5 people were killed in Gulf states. Their airspace was closed.

**Assessment:** Covered in detail in Case Study #1 (`proof/IRAN_STRIKES_2026.md`). Alliance fragmentation confirmed. **Confirmed.**

---

### 5. The China Gift

**What we said:** DEFENSE_FOREIGN_POLICY.md identified China as the *"most significant strategic challenge"* and called for $25 billion in additional Indo-Pacific investment and alliance strengthening to deter Chinese action.

**What happened:** The opposite occurred on every front:
- **Military**: Assets diverted to Iran theater and Gulf.
- **Diplomatic**: US credibility as an ally shattered — Foreign Affairs titled an article *"A Perfect Storm for Taiwan in 2026?"*
- **Economic**: Tariffs on allies (Japan, South Korea, Australia) weaken the economic partnerships that underpin military alliances.
- **Strategic**: China conducted its largest-ever Taiwan military exercise (December 2025) and rehearsed blockade tactics with 2,000 fishing vessels.

Every action that damages an alliance is a gift to Beijing. The administration delivered a year's worth of gifts in 12 months.

**Assessment:** **Confirmed.**
