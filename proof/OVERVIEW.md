## Methodology

### Scoring Predictions

Each prediction receives one of three statuses:

| Status | Definition | Standard of Evidence |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Confirmed** | The predicted event has occurred or is actively occurring in a form substantially matching the prediction. | Multiple credible news sources reporting the event; direct quotes or official actions matching predicted behavior. |
| **Emerging** | Early indicators suggest the prediction is materializing, but the full pattern has not yet resolved. | At least one credible indicator (market data, diplomatic statements, intelligence reporting) consistent with the prediction. |
| **Contradicted** | Evidence directly contradicts the prediction, or the predicted event failed to materialize within the expected timeframe. | Credible evidence of an alternative outcome; passage of the relevant time window without the predicted event occurring. |

### Rules

1. **No moving goalposts.** The prediction is evaluated against what was *actually written* in the source document, not post-hoc reinterpretation.
2. **Date-stamped.** Each case study records when the prediction was published and when the confirming event occurred.
3. **Source everything.** Confirming evidence must link to verifiable, public reporting.
4. **Admit misses.** Contradicted predictions are kept in the table, not removed. We learn from what we get wrong.
