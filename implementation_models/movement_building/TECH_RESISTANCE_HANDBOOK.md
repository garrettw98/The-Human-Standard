## 1. THE MISSION

You are a software engineer, data scientist, or researcher at a major technology company. You are paid well. You work on "cutting edge" problems.

But you have noticed the shift.
*   "Safety" teams are being dissolved.
*   "Defense" contracts are becoming "Domestic Surveillance" contracts.
*   Your CEO talks about "breaking democracy" or "exiting society" on podcasts.

**The Reality:** You are not building the future. You are building the infrastructure of a Corporate Monarchy.
**The Opportunity:** You are the only ones who can stop it. The "Architects" (Thiel, Musk, etc.) have the capital, but **you have the keys.**

## 2. THE THREE PILLARS OF RESISTANCE

### I. The Refusal (Code Ethics)
*   **Principle:** Do not write code that cannot be audited by the people it governs.
*   **Action:**
    *   **The "Black Box" Strike:** Refuse to work on government decision-making systems that are not Open Source (Tier 1).
    *   **The "Lethality" Line:** Refuse to work on autonomous kill chains (Anduril/Palantir contracts) that lack human-in-the-loop hardstops.
    *   **Collective Bargaining:** Use your leverage. A strike by 50 senior AI engineers hurts more than a strike by 50,000 warehouse workers. They cannot replace you easily.

### II. The Leak (Radical Transparency)
*   **Principle:** If the public knew the roadmap, they would revolt. Therefore, the roadmap must be public.
*   **Action:**
    *   **Safety Signals:** If your company claims a model is "safe" but internal benchmarks show it isn'tâ€”leak the benchmarks.
    *   **The "Cabal" Comms:** Leak the internal memos where executives discuss "regulatory capture," "post-democratic" goals, or "overcoming" public will.
    *   **Operational Security:** Use Tails OS, burner phones, and physical dead drops. Never leak from a work device. (See *Appendix A: Digital Self-Defense*).

### III. The Poison Pill (Open Source Permanence)
*   **Principle:** Make the tools impossible to monopolize.
*   **Action:**
    *   **Weight Liberation:** If a "Closed" model is actually dangerous to hoard, leak the weights (judiciously).
    *   **License Viralization:** Use copyleft licenses (AGPL) for critical infrastructure code to prevent proprietary capture.
    *   **Decentralized Backups:** Ensure critical public data cannot be deleted by a single CEO's whim. Mirror everything.

## 3. SPECIFIC COUNTER-MEASURES

### Against "The Panopticon" (Palantir / Worldcoin)
*   **The Tactic:** "Data Poisoning."
*   **Implementation:** Build and distribute tools that inject noise into biometric and behavioral data streams. Make the surveillance data worthless by lowering its signal-to-noise ratio.

### Against "The Red Caesar" (Gov-Corp)
*   **The Tactic:** "Bureaucratic Friction."
*   **Implementation:** If you are building tools for the "Schedule F" purge (automated firing systems), introduce "Fairness Constraints" that force human review. Slow down the automation of tyranny. "Malicious Compliance" with safety standards is your friend.

### Against "The Exit" (Network States)
*   **The Tactic:** "Infrastructure Dependency."
*   **Implementation:** Ensure that "Sovereign" tech stacks cannot function without the public internet/grid. Don't build the "kill switch" that lets them disconnect from the common good.
